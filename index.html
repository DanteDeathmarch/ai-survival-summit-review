AI Survival Summit Review: Is the $7 Event Actually Worth It?

Russell Brunson is speaking at a $7 event. That's real — but the price isn't quite what the page says, and I'd rather tell you than have you find out on your next credit card statement.

Disclosure: This is an independent review, not the official event site. The links here are affiliate links — if you register through them I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. That's a reason to read me skeptically, so I've put the downsides in as much detail as the upsides.

What it is

A live, 100% virtual 2-day event — August 18–19, 2026, 10am–2pm CST — presented by AI Marketing World, the team behind the in-person AI conference in Dallas, and hosted by Austin Armstrong, CEO of the AI video platform Syllaby.

Their tagline, and the whole thesis: "AI is splitting every industry into two groups. The replaced. And the paid."

⚠️ The real price: $7/month, not $7 once

The sales page says "$7." What you're buying is access through their AI Mavericks Skool community, which bills $7/month.

You can cancel anytime — Skool memberships aren't contracts. But if you register and forget about it, it rebills. They're not exactly hiding it (the page does say "in our $7 skool group"), but "$7" reads as a one-time ticket to most people, and it isn't one.

For what it's worth, the community isn't a bad thing to keep for a month — monthly AI workflows, a prompt and tool library, coaching calls. Just make that decision on purpose instead of by accident.

The other catch: it's a funnel, and they say so

Credit where it's due — they print the business model right on the page. Free tickets attract no-shows, so $7 buys enough skin in the game that you show up. And the whole event is an "audition" for AI Marketing World, their in-person conference.

So expect pitching toward the October event. They also state: "No forced upsell to unlock the 'real' content. Seven bucks buys both days." That's a more honest disclosure than most $7 funnels bother with.

The guarantee

"Show up. Watch the first two sessions. If you don't honestly believe you got at least 10x your $7 in value, email us before Day 1 ends and we'll refund you on the spot."

Note the deadline — before Day 1 ends, not whenever you get around to it.

Verdict

Worth it if you want a sequenced starting point for making money with AI and you'll actually show up. One usable automation, or one step toward your first AI dollar, pays for it many times over.

Skip it if you're already monetizing AI — or if you know you're a collector who buys things to feel productive. Their own page says that second part too, which is a strange and slightly reassuring thing for a sales page to admit.
